# Illustration Rules

Guidelines for composing illustration scenes.

---

## 1. Composition Rules
- **Rule of Thirds**: Position the mascot along the grid lines or intersections to create balanced weight.
- **Visual Flow**: Ensure characters look towards objects or arrows to guide the viewer's eyes.
- **Negative Space**: Maintain at least 40% empty space (pure white background) to keep the layout feeling professional.

---

## 2. Mascot Positioning
- **Emotion & Eyes**: Use simple changes in eye placement and spacing to show focus, surprise, or learning.
- **Limb Expression**: Keep arms and legs minimal. To show "holding", draw a simple line overlapping the object. No complex fingers.
- **Interaction**: The mascot should be actively engaged with the scene—reading a book, typing on a laptop, pointing at a blackboard.

---

## 3. Arrow & Flow Overlays
- When explaining sequences (like a restaurant waiter walking back and forth), use simple dashed black lines with arrowheads.
- Keep flow paths simple; avoid crossing paths.
- Add small, clean labels near arrows if needed.
