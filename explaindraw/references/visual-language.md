# Visual language

Apply these locks to illustrations, diagrams, and hybrid compositions.

## Palette and canvas

- Use a flat pure-white `#FFFFFF` background.
- Use black or near-black `#111111` lines and text.
- Use yellow `#FFC21A` as the only accent color.
- Do not add gradients, shadows, paper texture, colored scenery, decorative frames, or unrelated background elements.
- Preserve generous negative space and one clear visual hierarchy.

## Mascot identity

- Draw one continuous vertical egg/potato-shaped white body. Never create a separate head and torso.
- Draw two upright white oval eyes with large black pupils and two small white reflections in each pupil.
- Add thin curved eyebrows and a small black curved smile.
- Keep the face clean: no blush, makeup, nose, or ears.
- Draw thin, slightly imperfect black arms with four-finger hands.
- Draw thin black legs ending in flat black feet.
- Place exactly two small organic yellow paint marks vertically on the chest. Never replace them with buttons, code symbols, gears, badges, or topic icons.
- Add clothing only when essential to the action. Prefer handheld accessories.

## Drawing character

- Use minimalist editorial hand-drawn line art.
- Keep outlines thin, slightly wobbly, and visibly drawn rather than mechanically perfect.
- Avoid photorealism, 3D rendering, glossy vectors, clip art, and complex shading.
- Keep text sparse, short, correctly spelled, and presentation-legible.
